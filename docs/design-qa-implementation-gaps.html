Design QA · Architect of Suspicion · Internal

The implementation has drifted.

A field report on where the production app diverges from redesign/cinematic-forge — and how to bring it back. Five screens audited; thirteen gaps catalogued; a punch list at the end.

Reviewed April 2026 Branch redesign/cinematic-forge Audited Summons, Mansion, Chamber, Verdict, Architect Panel Severity 4 critical, 6 high, 3 medium
Thesis

The implementation reads correct — bone on ink, the right fonts, the right copy. But it has lost the theatre. Every screen is one or two clicks away from the POC, and the cumulative effect is a courtroom drama dressed as a CRUD app. Below, in priority order, the work to put the gavel back in the room.

01

The witness card never made the leap to parchment.

Critical

This is the single most consequential gap. The POC inverted the card — bone parchment with dark ink, an editorial broadsheet. The implementation kept the original dark slab. Everything downstream (stamps, queue, levers) is calibrated against the new card and reads wrong without it.

Implementation · today

Dark slab card, cream text on near-black.

WitnessCard.svelte uses background: linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(20,20,23,0.92), rgba(11,11,13,0.96)) with bone text. The card disappears into the chamber background and reads as a panel, not a document.

src/lib/components/WitnessCard.svelte · lines 80–90
POC · target

Bone parchment broadsheet, ink-colored text, ember edge rule.

.witness-card { background: var(--bone); color: var(--paper-ink); } with a 3px ember left rule and 32px box-shadow. Reads as a sworn document on a dark stage — the lit object in the room.

styles.css · .witness-card (~line 1190)
Fix
  • Swap card background to var(--color-bone); text color to var(--color-paper-ink) (a new token: #14141a).
  • Replace .wc-edge 1px inset border with a 3px ember left rule (::before, full height, no inset).
  • Drop the dashed brass border under .wc-head; use a solid 1px rgba(20,20,26,0.15) hairline instead. Bone needs ink hairlines, not brass.
  • Title goes to 52px Instrument Serif, weight 400 (regular, not italic) — the broadsheet effect requires the upright serif at scale.
  • Body copy: 17px / 1.55, color rgba(20,20,26,0.78), max-width: 56ch.
  • Padding bumps to 44px 56px 36px. The current 2rem/2.25rem reads cramped on bone.
02

The verdict stamps are vestigial.

Critical

In the POC a stamp is the punctuation of a decision — it lands hard, in the corner, in the language of evidence rooms. The implementation places it center-card at 25% top, in cream-on-black, where it reads as a watermark over a panel rather than ink slammed onto a document.

Implementation · today

Centered, cream / cyan / brass on dark, 4rem.

Without the bone card behind it, the stamp has no surface to stamp onto. The colors (bone, cyan-ink, brass-dim) all sit at similar luminance against the dark slab, so the stamp blends instead of slams.

WitnessCard.svelte · .wc-stamp (~line 138)
POC · target

Top-right, 22px, ‑8° rotation, on bone parchment.

Proof in deep green (#0a6a4a), Objection in ember, Struck in slate. Each scaled-in with a 180ms cubic-bezier overshoot to feel like a hand pressing a stamp. The contrast against bone is what sells it.

styles.css · .wc-stamp (~line 1260)
Fix
  • Move stamps to top: 36px; right: 44px; transform-origin: top right.
  • Use the POC's color palette: Proof #0a6a4a, Objection --ember, Dismiss #5a5a5a. Cyan-ink is wrong here — objection is the hot color.
  • Stamp size 22px Instrument Serif weight 700, letter-spacing 0.22em, uppercase, 3px solid border (not 6px double).
  • Animation: 180ms ease-out opacity + cubic-bezier(.3, 1.5, .4, 1) scale from 1.2 → 1.0 with ‑8° rotation. The slight overshoot is what makes it feel like a hand.
  • Hold stamp 360ms before card exits — the player needs to read it.
03

The levers are buttons, not levers.

High

The POC treats the three actions as instrument-panel switches — tall slate panels with brass borders, color encoding the verdict on hover. The implementation has flat, low-contrast pills that feel like form controls.

Implementation · today

Flat panels, hover only changes border color.

Border rgba(233,228,216,0.12) hover to 0.4 — barely perceptible. Background stays the same. No glow, no shadow. They feel optional.

POC · target

Embossed slate switches with verdict-colored glow on hover.

Background linear-gradient(180deg, #22262f, #14171f), 1px brass border, padding 14×12. On hover, glow + 1px ring in the verdict color: brass for proof, ember for objection, brass-dim for dismiss. Click feels like throwing a switch.

Fix
  • Lever background: linear-gradient(180deg, #22262f, #14171f); border 1px solid rgba(196,162,78,0.35) — this is the one place warm brass survives, because the levers are the only physical mechanism in the chamber.
  • Hover state per-verdict: ember box-shadow on objection, bone-glow on proof, brass-dim on dismiss. box-shadow: 0 0 0 1px var(--brass), 0 8px 24px rgba(...).
  • Add the keyboard hint visibly (P →, ↑ O, ← ⌫) in mono 9px, brass-dim. The implementation has them already; they just need to read like they belong on an instrument.
  • Hint copy under each lever: 11px Geist, paper-dim. Strike from record / Counter-evidence / Enter as evidence.
04

The summons title is whispering.

High

clamp(2.5rem, 6vw, 4.25rem) ceilings the headline at 68px. The POC ran it at 56–72px uppercase serif with 0.25em tracking. The whole thing is meant to read like a marquee. At 68px italic without tracking, it reads as a hero header.

Implementation · today

Italic headline, no tracking, ember of small.

The current treatment makes of brass-dim and small (0.75em). In the POC, of is the only ember on the screen and it is given equal optical weight to Architect / Suspicion — that's the whole joke of the title.

routes/+page.svelte · .summons-headline (~line 226)
POC · target

Roman caps, 56px, 0.25em tracking, ember of sized as a peer.

Letter-spacing carries the marquee feel; size carries the menace. The ember of stays the same size as the rest of the line, italic, as the single chromatic event in an otherwise bone-on-ink composition.

Fix
  • Headline: font-size: clamp(48px, 5.2vw, 64px); letter-spacing: 0.22em; text-transform: uppercase; font-style: normal.
  • .summons-amp: keep ember color, remove the 0.75em sizing, add font-style: italic. It should read at the same x-height as its neighbors.
  • Add the under-glow back: text-shadow: 0 0 30px rgba(210,58,42,0.18), 0 2px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.8). The ember halo is what tells the eye this is the cinematic version.
05

The "Enter the Mansion" CTA is a web button.

High
Implementation · today

Translucent ember pill, italic body label.

background: linear-gradient(rgba(210,58,42,0.18), rgba(138,31,20,0.18)). Reads as a primary CTA in a SaaS app. The dossier is set in 1920; the button should be too.

POC · target

Embossed brass-bordered lever, uppercase tracked label, key icon.

Dark substrate, brass 1px border, inner highlight + outer drop shadow, all-caps display serif 13px / 0.28em. A key icon sits to the left. On hover the brass glows.

Fix
  • Background: linear-gradient(180deg, #2a2417 0%, #14110a 100%). Border 1px solid var(--color-bone-dim).
  • Box-shadow: inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,230,170,0.2), inset 0 -1px 0 rgba(0,0,0,0.6), 0 6px 20px rgba(0,0,0,0.6).
  • Label: font-family: var(--font-display); font-size: 13px; font-weight: 600; letter-spacing: 0.28em; text-transform: uppercase.
  • Add a 22×22 SVG key icon on the left (circle r=4 cx=8 cy=12 + 3 rectangles for the bit). Currently nowhere in the implementation.
  • Hover glow: 0 0 24px rgba(240,194,77,0.25) — this is one of the rare moments warm gold is allowed; the lever is physical.
06

The mansion is a photograph, not a board.

High

The POC treats the exterior image as something pinned to a board — a 6px black inset shadow gives it depth, brass corners hint at framing, and the room pins read as physical objects on top. The implementation shows the photograph at full-bleed with a thin border. It reads flat.

Fix
  • Wrap the image in a .board with inset: 26px, brass 1px border, and box-shadow: inset 0 0 0 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.5), 0 30px 80px rgba(0,0,0,0.7). The black inset is the trick — it makes the photo feel set into something.
  • Pins: 16×16 brass radial dots (radial-gradient(circle at 35% 30%, #f0c24d, #8a7235 65%, #3a2f18 100%)) with two pinging rings (::before and ::after, animated scale(0.6) → scale(1.6) over 2.5s, staggered 0.7s).
  • Tags: brass 1px border, dark gradient background, backdrop-filter: blur(2px), leader line connecting to the dot.
  • Visited state: brass dot becomes saturated gold; box border brass-glow. Sealed: ember rim.
07

The attention meter is over-decorated.

Medium

The implementation's gauge is closer to the POC than I expected — tick marks present, hub correct, needle in ember. Two real issues: the arc gradient runs grey-to-bone (cold) when it should run cold-to-warm to encode escalation, and the tick labels (Drifting / Watching / Interested / Riveted) below the gauge fight with the glyph for attention. POC labels are inside the arc itself.

Fix
  • Arc gradient: #3a3a42 → #c9c4b4 → #d23a2a (slate → bone → ember). Mood is geographic on the arc — left is cold, right is hot.
  • Move mood labels inside the arc: COOL at 24,146 / ENGAGED at 130,36 (top of arc) / FURY at 236,146. Drop the .meter-track grid below.
  • Below the gauge, render a single readout line: {mood} · {value}/100 in JetBrains Mono, ember.
  • Add the per-pick delta callout below: a 10.5px mono pill that fades in for 350ms on attention change, color-coded (ember for up, cyan for down, brass-dim for flat). The implementation has the smoothed value but doesn't surface the delta — players miss the consequence of their pick.
08

The record reads as a chat log.

Medium

Feed entries in the implementation share one visual rhythm. The POC differentiates: actions get a cyan-ink left rule and a small diamond marker; the Architect's reactions get an ember left rule, an ember diamond, and switch to italic Instrument Serif at 15.5px. The mode-switch in typography is what sells the Architect speaking.

Fix
  • Action entry: border-left: 2px solid var(--color-cyan-ink), ::before diamond marker (8×8 rotated 45°), body in Geist 14px / 1.4.
  • Reaction entry: border-left: 2px solid var(--color-ember), ::before diamond with box-shadow: 0 0 8px var(--color-ember), body in Instrument Serif italic 15.5px color #e8d8b8.
  • Time stamp above body in mono 9px, brass-dim, with chamber slug for action entries (23:12 · PARLOR).
09

Proof and Objection look like the same thing.

Medium

In the POC, the tally boxes are color-coded by verdict: Proof reads in bone-glow, Objection in ember. The implementation shows both numerals in the same paper color, which collapses the distinction the entire game is built on.

Fix
  • .tally-box.proof .n { color: var(--color-bone); }
  • .tally-box.objection .n { color: var(--color-ember); }
  • Numeral: 28px Instrument Serif weight 700, baseline-aligned with a small 10px mono collected caption.
10

The dossier corners are the wrong color and the wrong size.

High

Implementation: 14×14 ember corners. POC: 38×38 brass-dim corners. The POC corners are deliberately understated — they're a printer's mark, not an alarm. Making them ember at 14px reads as warning state, which is exactly the wrong note for a calm legal document.

Fix
  • Corner ornaments: width: 38px; height: 38px; border: 1px solid var(--color-brass-dim); — not ember.
  • Position them top/left: -1px (flush with dossier edge), not 6px inset.
  • Restore the giant serif quotation marks framing the claim: ::before { content: '"'; font-size: 72px; top: 18px; left: ‑36px; color: var(--color-bone-dim); } and matching ::after bottom-right. Currently absent — the claim sits as a plain blockquote.
11

The verdict screen's actions are unstyled.

High

The cover letter itself is close to the POC — a real win. But the four buttons below it (Read Resume / Download Letter / Share Record / Begin Again) are flat browser buttons, which collapses the ceremony of the whole screen.

Fix
  • Treat all four as quiet ghost buttons: 1px rgba(196,162,78,0.3) border, transparent background, 13px Instrument Serif weight 600, 0.22em tracking, uppercase.
  • Download Letter is the primary — same chrome as the Summons lever button: dark substrate, brass border, inner highlight, key icon (or wax-seal icon).
  • Spacing: 14px between buttons, 28px above the row, separated from the letter by a hairline brass rule.
  • Hover on any: brass border brightens to --color-bone, subtle 0 0 16px brass glow.
12

The resume sidebar is bolted on.

Medium

It exists, it has the right tokens, but it lives in its own typographic world — tighter, more SaaS, no ornament. The verdict screen is the one place the player gets the recruiter-friendly content; the resume should feel like an exhibit appendix, not a panel.

Fix
  • Wrap the resume in a parchment-style container that echoes the witness card: bone background, ink text, 3px ember left rule, 1px hairline divider.
  • Section labels (Experience / Skills / Education) in Instrument Serif italic 12px with a thin brass underline — not all-caps mono.
  • Skills as small ink-on-bone tags with a hairline border, not the dark cards the implementation uses.
  • Drop the disclaimer line; it breaks the fourth wall in the wrong way for this screen.
13

The on-deck card is missing.

High

The POC stacks the next witness card behind the active one, scaled to 0.94 with 0.55 opacity and a slight grayscale, so the player feels the queue physically. After a verdict, the active card animates out and the peek promotes into position. Without this, the chamber loses its rhythm; each card arrives without anticipation.

Fix
  • Render cards[i+1] alongside the active card with class .peek: transform: translate(32px, 36px) scale(0.94); opacity: 0.55; filter: grayscale(0.35) brightness(0.85);
  • Hide the peek card's body and stamps (.wc-body, .wc-stamp { display: none; }) — only the masthead is visible.
  • On verdict, cross-fade: active card animates out per its verdict (proof rises, objection shudders + falls right, dismiss slides left + drains to grey); peek card promotes to translate(0,0) scale(0.985) with delay 120ms.
  • This animation is the chamber's signature beat — it sells the witness format. It is the highest-leverage interaction-design fix on this list.

Order of operations

  1. Land the parchment witness card. Everything in the chamber depends on this: stamps, levers, queue, animations. Fix #01 first; the rest of the chamber follows in hours.
  2. Restore the verdict stamps. Right corner, ‑8°, the three-color palette. Highest visible signal of decision per pick.
  3. Add the on-deck peek card. The animation choreography only works once the queue is visible.
  4. Rebuild the lever buttons. Slate gradient, brass border, verdict-colored hover glow. Includes the keyboard-hint typography pass.
  5. Summons title + lever button. These are the two pieces a viewer judges in the first 800ms; they determine whether the rest of the app gets a fair read.
  6. Mansion board treatment. The 6px black inset shadow + brass corners. Pins get the double-ring ping animation.
  7. Architect panel polish. Gauge gradient cold-to-warm, mood labels inside arc, feed typography mode-switch, color-coded tally.
  8. Dossier corners and quotation marks. Quick win — ten lines of CSS, large perceived effect.
  9. Verdict screen actions and resume sidebar. Final 10% of polish; saves the screen from feeling like a wrap-up.
